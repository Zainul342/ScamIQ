# 🚀 ScamIQ — Demo Script & Submission Preparation

A concise, end-to-end guide for recording your buildathon demo, delivering a clear narrative, and preparing your submission materials. Follow this flow to keep the story tight, impactful, and memorable.

---

## 🎬 1) Demo Video Flow (3 Minutes)

### 1. Opening Hook (0:00–0:15)
**Say:**
> “Most people never get scam training unless they work at a company. ScamIQ turns scam detection into a fast, arcade-style game anyone can play.”

**Show:**
- Landing page (clean load)
- Product name + tagline
- Primary CTA (**Play Now**)

**Tip:** Start with energy. Keep it human, not technical.

---

### 2. Problem Setup (0:15–0:30)
**Say:**
> “Scams are everywhere now—SMS, email, DMs, fake login pages, even AI-generated messages. I wanted to make scam awareness fun, fast, and shareable.”

**Show:**
- Quick visual sweep of channels (SMS, email, DM, login)
- Transition into the core promise: *Play → Learn → Share*

---

### 3. Core Gameplay (0:30–1:30)
**Click:**
- **Play Now**
- Play through 3–4 rounds
- Include at least: 1 SMS, 1 email, 1 DM/marketplace

**Narration Tips:**
- Think out loud (“This feels urgent… link looks odd…”) 
- Intentionally get **one answer wrong** to demonstrate feedback
- Keep pacing brisk; don’t over-explain

**Say:**
> “You don’t just get told the answer—you get red flags, a short explanation, and a practical tip you can use right away.”

---

### 4. Scoring & Results (1:30–2:10)
**Show:**
- Final **ScamIQ score**
- Badge reveal
- Weakest category insight
- Share card

**Say:**
> “The result isn’t just a score. You get a ScamIQ badge, your weak spot, and a shareable result.”

---

### 5. AI Layer (2:10–2:35)
**Show:**
- AI Challenge Pack
- AI Weakness Coach
- (Optionally) Fallback behavior

**Say:**
> “ScamIQ uses AI for challenge generation and personalized coaching—with a curated fallback so it stays reliable even if the API fails.”

---

### 6. Closing (2:35–3:00)
**Say:**
> “ScamIQ makes scam awareness feel like a game, not a lecture. It’s consumer-first, mobile-friendly, and built to spread through sharing.”

**Show:**
- Share card (final frame)
- Project link / CTA

**End strong. Pause for a clean final frame.**

---

## 🎯 2) What to Emphasize

- **Consumer-first:** Not enterprise training
- **Fast loop:** 8 rounds in < 3 minutes
- **Educational:** Instant feedback every round
- **Shareable:** ScamIQ score card
- **Reliable:** Static fallback without API
- **AI-enabled:** Scenario generation + coaching

---

## 💥 3) Emotional Impact Tips

- Open with a real truth: scams are common and evolving
- Create tension: “Is this safe or a scam?”
- Show one mistake to make feedback meaningful
- End with the share card to reinforce social value
- Keep tone confident, simple, and human

## ❓ 5) Optional FAQ

**What is ScamIQ?**  
A fast, arcade-style game that teaches users to spot real-world scams.

**Who is it for?**  
Everyday internet users without formal security training.

**What makes it different?**  
Consumer-first, mobile-friendly, shareable—and designed as a game, not a dashboard.

**How is AI used?**  
For generating scenarios and personalized coaching, with a static fallback.

**What if AI fails?**  
The app falls back to a curated dataset so gameplay continues smoothly.
